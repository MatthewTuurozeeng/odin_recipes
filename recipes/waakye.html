Description

Waakye is a popular Ghanaian dish made with rice and beans, traditionally cooked with dried millet leaves (called waakye leaves) which give it a distinctive brown color and unique flavor. It's typically served with accompaniments like fried plantains, boiled eggs, spaghetti (locally called 'Talia'), garri, shito (black pepper sauce), wele (cow skin), fish, or meat.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of Rice
  • Beans
  • Waakye leaves
  • Salt and Water

Steps to Make Waakye

  1. Boil the beans
    • Wash and boil the black-eyed peas or red beans in a large pot with waakye leaves. Cook until the beans are soft. (Remove waakye leaves after the color is released.)
  2. Add rice
    • Wash the rice and add it to the pot of beans.
  3. Add Salt
    • Season with salt to taste.
  4. Cook together
    • Allow the rice and beans to cook together until the water is absorbed and the rice is soft.
  5. Serve
    • Waakye is typically served with your choice of side dishes like stew, shito, spaghetti, fried plantain, boiled eggs, and meat or fish.
